About me

I am an aspiring science communicator. This passion stems from the emerging societal need to cultivate a scientifically-literate population outside traditional learning environments. As such, I obtained a diploma in Mass Communication to develop my communication skills while building professional connections. Additionally, I produce various science communication videos via my YouTube channel to develop my competencies.

I believe that pursuing a double degree in Mathematics and Philosophy will allow me to achieve my goal. Mathematics is the language of the universe. It has far-reaching applications in various fields of science and technology. Philosophy builds the rigour of thinking by systematically internalising abstract concepts and integrating knowledge into real-world projects. The academic background in both subjects will allow me to effectively communicate a wide range of complex topics to any audience.

Why science communication

Our home is on fire, and we're running out of time. Reversing climate change requires cooperation between countries, corporations and individuals worldwide. We need to eliminate dependency on fossil fuels while developing clean, sustainable technology to support our way of life.

Singapore is in a unique position. As a regional business hub, we have the capacity to invest and promote the development of sustainable technology. As a small nation, we are able to test solutions and become a model for other countries.

Science communication equips individuals, corporations and agencies with the understanding of relevant data so that effective policies and decisions can be made. Furthermore, a science-literate population is better equipped to address complex societal issues through analytical thinking. Because science improves the quality of life, I want to become a professional science communicator so that more people can benefit aesthetically and functionally.

This included the data scrapping of journalists' contacts for media lists and the automatic scheduling for social media posts. It allowed us to focus on items that required our attention, such as the crafting of proposals and releases.

Media entrepreneurship

During my media entrepreneurship module, my teammates and I created a business proposal and video pitch. The module required us to conceptualise a successful media business. This included primary and secondary research, 5-year financial projections, traditional business analysis and pitches. We proposed a social-networking platform called Venture Bubble.
