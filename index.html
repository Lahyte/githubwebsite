Science Communication

Our home is on fire, and we're running out of time. Reversing climate change requires cooperation between countries, corporations and individuals worldwide. We need to eliminate dependency on fossil fuels while developing clean, sustainable technology to support our way of life.

Singapore is in a unique position. As a regional business hub, we have the capacity to invest and promote the development of sustainable technology. As a small nation, we are able to test solutions and become a model for other countries.

Science communication equips individuals, corporations and agencies with the understanding of relevant data so that effective policies and decisions can be made. Furthermore, a science literate population is better equipped to address complex societal issues through analytical thinking. Because science improves the quality of life, I want to become a professional science communicator so that more people can benefit aesthetically and functionally.

Degree Outlook

Science communication requires critical thinking and a clear understanding of complex situations in order to craft effective media messages. The rigor of a University degree will sharpen my mind further and connect me with bright, passionate individuals who want to build an informed society.

Diploma in Mass Communication

I chose to study Mass Communication in Ngee Ann Polytechnic because I wanted the skills and knowledge of a media practitioner. As a graduate, I am capable of working in any aspect of the industry, from traditional and digital production, to journalism and marketing. My industry exposure in Year 3 allowed me to better craft media messages in a real-world setting. With this, I am able to communicate the science that I will learn on a University level and through work experience.
