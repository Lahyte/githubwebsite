When I was 10, I decided I want to change the world.

When I was 14, I began to realise the world was big. Too big for one person to fix. I began finding people. People who could somehow change the world.

When I was 17, I decided that my reasoning was flawed. The world was too big. The problems we face are just too much for any person or any team I could find.

Over the years, I made many connections. People taught me that my world view was idealistic. Change is not easy. I decided that hope resides in the future generation.

I’ve always felt that my calling was in changing lives. I’ve always had the desire to help people live the best lives they could. Somewhere along the way, I decided I’m not good enough to change the world directly. So I set my sights on indirect change.

Progress comes at a price for individuals, societies and the environment. I’m scared we don’t have enough resources to save ourselves as a species.

Being alive today is crazy. What are we supposed to think? There is too much happening for anyone to keep track of a worldview.

I’m placing my bet on teaching. Nurturing people who could be better than me. It’s an absurdly small chance, but it’s possible.
