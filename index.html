Concerns


See my full write up here.




1. Cliamte change

We have a million concerns in life, and while climate change is imperative, it is deprioritised by more immediate threats. Adapting climate action to Maslow's hierarchy of needs, climate action is on par with self-actualisation. This is why I am concerned with climate change — it threatens our survival as a species, tests our metal to organise on a global level, and requires enough self-actualised individuals to lead the charge.



2. Active citizenry

Active citizenry indicates an engaged population that knows its priorities and concerns, allowing society to act on climate change, social mobility, education, healthcare and so much more.



3. Social mobility

People's livelihoods are tied to their socio-economic status. It is the capacity of the people — what they can and cannot do. Those in the low-income brackets are unable to care about climate change, active citizenry, healthcare and more because all their time and energy is devoted into earning their keep for their household. Education and job prospects are rightfully their top priority, regardless of the imperativeness of every other world issue for without their basic needs being met, they will die. This is why I'm concerned with social mobility — because it may tear the fabric of society apart, and it will determine a population's concerns and priorities, and most of all, it will determine the quality of lives people will have to experience for as long as they remain in that bracket.



4. Mental healthcare

This is why climate action is difficult. There are many prerequisites to such high-intensity improvements: a comfortable socio-economic status, active participation in society, good mental health, and the time, energy and will to improve or enact change. I am concerned about the mental health landscape in Singapore because it impacts our daily lives and beyond.



5. YouTube

It has become a platform where people can express themselves to the world. People don't have to just sit on ideas, they can take what's in their head and put it in a medium for anyone in the world to see, to create, to verbalise, to transmit ideas across from one person to another. If someone had a novel idea, it could be written and published online. If someone had a composition, they could play it for the world. And if someone had a brilliant idea to entertain, they could do it. Because the Internet is free for all to use how they see fit, and if they want to share their ideas with the world, they can do it.
